# ## Real-time Monitoring of CO‚ÇÇ Dissolution Rate & Caprock Integrity via Multi-Agent Acoustic Emission Analysis and Deep Learning-Based Predictive Modeling

**Abstract:** This paper presents a novel methodology for real-time monitoring of Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) operations, specifically focusing on the dissolution rate of injected CO‚ÇÇ and the long-term integrity of overlying caprock formations. Our approach leverages a distributed network of micro-acoustic emission (AE) sensors, coupled with a deep learning-based predictive model operating on a hyperdimensional vector space representation of AE data. This allows for continuous, high-resolution assessment of fluid-rock interactions, early detection of caprock failure precursors, and proactive mitigation strategies, exceeding the capabilities of traditional monitoring techniques. The system is immediately commercializable and optimized for practical engineering application.

**1. Introduction:**

The increasing urgency of climate change mitigation necessitates widespread deployment of CCS technologies. However, ensuring the long-term safety and reliability of CO‚ÇÇ storage sites is paramount. Existing monitoring methods, such as periodic seismic surveys and well logging, are often infrequent and lack the temporal resolution required to detect subtle changes indicative of caprock instability or anomalous CO‚ÇÇ dissolution. This research introduces a scalable, real-time monitoring system that combines advanced acoustic emission sensing with deep learning-based predictive modeling to address these limitations. Our system focuses on monitoring transient fluid-rock interactions, crucial for early failure detection.

**2. Background & Related Work:**

Acoustic emission (AE) is a technique that detects elastic waves generated by micro-fractures and deformation within a material. In the context of CO‚ÇÇ storage, AE can provide direct insights into the processes of CO‚ÇÇ dissolution, rock fracturing, and permeability changes. Previous research has utilized AE for caprock monitoring, but often relied on threshold-based event detection and manual interpretation. Deep learning has demonstrated promise in analyzing AE data, but often lack real-time operational capabilities. Our novelty lies in integrating a distributed, multi-agent sensor network with a dynamically updating deep learning model operating in a high-dimensional space, allowing for continuous, autonomous monitoring and predictive assessment.

**3. Methodology: Multi-Agent Acoustic Emission Network and Hyperdimensional Representation**

Our system comprises a network of strategically placed micro-AE sensors distributed within and around the caprock. These sensors continuously record AE signals, which are then pre-processed using adaptive bandpass filtering to remove noise and isolate relevant frequency bands (typically 20-100 kHz). The pre-processed data streams are not directly fed into the deep learning model. Instead, they are transformed into hyperdimensional vectors via a random projection technique.

**4. Deep Learning-Based Predictive Model: Recurrent Hyperdimensional Neural Network (RHNN)**

The composite hypervectors are fed into a Recurrent Hyperdimensional Neural Network (RHNN). RHNNs are particularly well-suited for time-series analysis and prediction because their hyperdimensional representation enables efficient processing of sequential data. The RHNN architecture consists of multiple layers of hyperdimensional processing units, each layer incorporating a non-linear activation function.

The RHNN is trained using a reinforcement learning (RL) approach, where the reward signal is based on the accuracy of predicting future caprock integrity metrics (e.g., fracture density, permeability, Young‚Äôs modulus).

**5. Experimental Design and Data Analysis**
