# ## Enhanced Bio-Polymer Scaffold Fabrication via Dynamic Morphology Control using Microfluidic Reactive Crystallization for Bone Regeneration

**Abstract:** This research proposes a novel fabrication technique for poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA) scaffolds optimized for bone regeneration, utilizing microfluidic reactive crystallization coupled with dynamic control of polymer morphology. Current PLGA scaffolds often lack the requisite porosity and interconnectedness for effective cell infiltration and vascularization. We introduce a system that leverages rapid mixing within a microfluidic device, inducing controlled PLGA crystallization in the presence of a biocompatible crosslinking agent, allowing for real-time manipulation of scaffold pore size and interconnectivity. This method demonstrates statistically significant improvement in osteoblast adhesion and proliferation compared to conventional solvent casting techniques, presenting a commercially viable route to enhanced bone regenerative materials.

**1. Introduction:**

Bone regeneration remains a significant clinical challenge, particularly in the treatment of large bone defects resulting from trauma, disease or surgical resection. PLGA, due to its biodegradability and biocompatibility, is a widely used material in bone tissue engineering. However, traditional methods like solvent casting and particulate leaching often produce scaffolds with inadequate mechanical properties, limited porosity, and poor interconnectivity hindering cell infiltration and vascularization – essential components for successful bone regeneration. This research addresses these shortcomings by introducing a microfluidic reactive crystallization method offering precise control over PLGA scaffold morphology, thereby improving its ability to promote bone regeneration.

**2. Theoretical Background & Novelty:**

Current fabrication techniques lack the spatial control needed to precisely engineer the microarchitecture of PLGA scaffolds. Reactive crystallization, where crystallization is induced in the presence of a crosslinking agent, modifies polymer chain entanglement and can alter crystalline morphology. Microfluidics provides a unique platform for rapid mixing, temperature control, and precise reagent delivery, enabling controlled reactive crystallization kinetics. While reactive crystallization of polymers has been explored, its application in microfluidic systems for controlled scaffold fabrication and real-time manipulation of porosity remains largely unexplored. Our innovation lies in the dynamic feedback control of crystallization conditions within the microfluidic channel to precisely engineer scaffold porosity and interconnectivity, mapped to cell viability and bone formation.
